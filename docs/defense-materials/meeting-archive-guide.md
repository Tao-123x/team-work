# Teamwork Meeting Archive Guide

This guide organizes the paper evidence into one presentation-ready structure for the five agreed key meetings.

## Core Rule

Use **five meetings only**:

1. Week 1 project selection
2. Week 2 pivot and re-scoping
3. Week 3 integration and issue review
4. Week 4 lifecycle completion and verification
5. Meeting 5 final deployment, rehearsal, and defense preparation

Do not create a sixth meeting.

## Recommended Physical Structure

Do **not** bind every page into one huge stack.

Use three physical layers:

1. `Main Archive Pack`
2. `Individual Handwritten Packets`
3. `Reference Pack`

This keeps the evidence complete without making the live defense chaotic.

## Layer 1: Main Archive Pack

This is the pack that sits on the table and can be handed to the teacher first.

Recommended order:

1. Cover page
2. Short archive index
3. Problem domain sheet
4. Scoping and planning sheet
5. Meeting 1 divider
6. Meeting 1 key pages
7. Meeting 2 divider
8. Meeting 2 key pages
9. Meeting 3 divider
10. Meeting 3 key pages
11. Meeting 4 divider
12. Meeting 4 key pages
13. Meeting 5 divider
14. Meeting 5 key pages
15. Team leader report
16. Team meeting summary

Recommended content per meeting in the main pack:

- `PreMeeting_Shared_Base`
- `PostMeeting_Shared_Record`
- `PreMeeting_Team_Leader_Page`
- `PostMeeting_Team_Leader_Official_Record`

This means the main archive shows:

- what the meeting was supposed to achieve
- what was actually discussed and concluded
- how the team leader controlled the meeting

## Layer 2: Individual Handwritten Packets

Each member should keep a small personal packet in hand or in a separate transparent folder.

Each packet contains five pages only:

1. Week 1 personal handwritten sheet
2. Week 2 personal handwritten sheet
3. Week 3 personal handwritten sheet
4. Week 4 personal handwritten sheet
5. Meeting 5 personal handwritten sheet

Recommended owner mapping:

- `Jiacheng Tao`: Team Leader
- `Pengyang Liu`: Product Owner
- `Sicheng Wu`: Quality Controller
- `Xiaofeng Dang`: Development Member
- `Ruilin Ma`: Development Member

Important:

- Every member writes on their own `PreMeeting_*_Notes.docx` pages
- The Team Leader also writes the extra `PreMeeting_Team_Leader_Page.docx` for each meeting
- The personal packet is mainly for proof of real participation
- The main archive pack is mainly for teacher browsing

## Layer 3: Reference Pack

This pack does not need to be shown first.

Use it only if the teacher asks follow-up questions.

Recommended content:

- all `PostMeeting_*_Notes.docx` pages
- backend test summary
- architecture summary
- deployment notes
- demo script
- release / regression notes

## Cover Page Content

Keep the cover page simple:

- Group 15
- Project title
- Team roles
- QR code
- One-line project summary
- One-line note:
  `This archive contains handwritten and structured evidence of offline teamwork across five key meetings.`

## Meeting Priority

If the teacher only flips quickly, the most important order is:

1. Meeting 1
2. Meeting 2
3. Meeting 5
4. Meeting 4
5. Meeting 3

Why:

- Meeting 1 proves real discussion and selection
- Meeting 2 proves tutor-feedback pivot
- Meeting 5 proves final teamwork control before defense
- Meeting 4 proves the product became verifiable
- Meeting 3 proves mid-project reflection and issue awareness

## What To Print For Each Meeting

For each meeting, print:

- 1 `PreMeeting_Shared_Base`
- 1 `PostMeeting_Shared_Record`
- 1 `PreMeeting_Team_Leader_Page`
- 1 `PostMeeting_Team_Leader_Official_Record`
- 5 personal `PreMeeting_*_Notes`
- 5 personal `PostMeeting_*_Notes` if you want complete evidence

If you want a lighter teacher-facing version:

- keep all `PreMeeting_*_Notes` in personal packets
- keep all `PostMeeting_*_Notes` in the reference pack
- keep only the four core pages per meeting in the main archive

## What Each Person Should Handwrite

Use short real notes, not essays.

Each personal page should include four things:

1. what I focused on
2. what I suggested
3. what I agreed or disagreed with
4. what task or next step I took away

### Team Leader

Write about:

- meeting control
- decision quality
- role coordination
- what must be fixed before the next stage

### Product Owner

Write about:

- problem clarity
- scope control
- MVP boundary
- whether the story is easy to explain

### Quality Controller

Write about:

- risk points
- bugs or unstable behavior
- acceptance concerns
- what evidence is still missing

### Development Members

Write about:

- flow clarity
- logic stability
- page or route problems
- what needs to be improved for the next stage

## How Much To Handwrite

Target amount per page:

- `5 to 8 lines` is ideal
- `3 lines` is too thin
- a full page of dense writing looks less natural

The writing should look like real meeting notes:

- short phrases are fine
- arrows are fine
- mixed English and short Chinese notes are fine
- underlines and circles are fine

## Best Realistic Writing Style

Good examples:

- `Need clearer MVP boundary`
- `Tutor feedback means old topic too weak`
- `Keep demo short`
- `Need safer order flow`
- `Check QR access before defense`

Bad examples:

- long paragraphs
- identical handwriting content across all five members
- perfect polished reflection sentences on every page

## Printing Order Checklist

Before printing, check:

- all five meetings exist
- no Week 6 meeting is included
- all meeting files are black and white
- pre-meeting pages still have writable space
- post-meeting pages are reference-style, not blank forms
- names and roles are correct

## Fastest Practical Assembly

If time is limited, do this first:

1. Print the four core pages for each of the five meetings
2. Print the five personal `PreMeeting_*_Notes` pages for each member
3. Prepare Week 1 and Week 2 first
4. Prepare Meeting 5 next
5. Add the team leader report and team meeting summary last

## Suggested Live Use

During the defense:

- keep the `Main Archive Pack` on the desk
- let each member hold their own `Individual Handwritten Packet`
- if the teacher asks about teamwork:
  show Meeting 1, Meeting 2, and Meeting 5 first
- if the teacher asks about build progress:
  show Meeting 3 and Meeting 4 after that
